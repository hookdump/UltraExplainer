Concept · Clustering feedback

Two complaints about the same thing look far apart — until you measure the angle, not the distance.

A note mentioned 3 times and one mentioned 47 times point the same direction in embedding space. Euclidean distance says they're unrelated; cosine similarity says they're the same topic. That one choice decides whether your clusters make sense.

Embedding space (2-D slice)

Near-collinear, different length. Magnitude tracks how often; direction tracks what about. (lengths illustrative, not to scale)

The trap, and the fix
What you'd expect

Cluster by distance

Euclidean distance grows with magnitude, so a popular complaint drifts far from a rare wording of the same complaint. The clusterer splits one issue into two and under-counts severity.

What's true

Cluster by angle

Cosine ignores magnitude and compares direction. The two phrasings collapse into one cluster whose size reflects total volume — exactly what a triage queue needs.

Worked example
Feedback‖v‖Euclid → Acos → ACluster
“app is slow” item A, baseline1.00.001.00#slow
“painfully too slow lately”3.22.210.98#slow
“can't log in”1.11.380.12#auth

Row 2 is the farthest by Euclid yet nearest by cosine — the exact case that breaks distance-based clustering. It lands in #slow only because we compare angles.

Edge cases & caveats

• Zero vectors have no direction — cosine is undefined; guard with a magnitude floor.
• Very short text embeds noisily; cosine over-merges. Gate on a minimum token count.
• When magnitude is the signal (e.g. anomaly scoring), Euclidean is the right tool — this is not a universal win, it's a fit for topic clustering.
